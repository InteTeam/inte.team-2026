Case Study

We Built the Software That Runs Our Own Business.

Before we built anything for anyone else, we had the same problem most local businesses have: bookings on paper, updates over WhatsApp, no record of what was promised to who. Here's what we replaced it with — and why it's now the flagship of our web development work.

The Problem

Paper-based bookings don't scale past one person remembering everything.

Running electronics repairs out of paper tickets and WhatsApp chats meant no shared record between jobs, no automatic follow-up, and no way to see stock or job status without asking. Every new booking depended on someone being available to write it down correctly.

What We Built

Our own multi-tenant CRM — Book Repair Online.

  • Lead capture through to final invoicing, with nothing falling through the cracks between stages.
  • Business WhatsApp linked directly to tickets — any message becomes a trackable job.
  • Low-stock alerts so spare parts and devices are ready before they run out.
  • Self-hosted, white-labelled infrastructure — full control of our own data and uptime.

The Result

We use it every day, and it's what we now build for other local businesses.

It's not a demo environment or a sales prototype — it's the same system behind every repair we book. When we build a CRM, booking flow, or WhatsApp integration for a client, it's built on infrastructure we already trust with our own business.

It Didn't Stop There

As our customer base has grown, so has the platform underneath it.

CRM was the first piece, not the only one. Every one of these lives in the same ecosystem, built by our own team, day after day:

  • CMS — content management for shop websites
  • Store Front — e-commerce, built on the same customer data as the CRM
  • Warehouse & Logistics — stock and fulfilment across locations
  • Procurement — purchasing, handled inside the same platform
  • Nextcloud — private storage, calendars and contacts, connected in
  • oor-hq — how our own team runs projects, day to day

Automatic backups and 2FA run underneath all of it, with single sign-on tying it together so switching between systems feels like one product, not several. And everywhere a customer's own customers look, it's white-label — no Inte.Team logo in sight. Just their brand, running on our infrastructure.
