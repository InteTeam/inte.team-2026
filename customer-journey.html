The Framework Behind Every Solution We Build

One Blueprint.
Every Business.

Whether you sell car repairs, legal advice, or SaaS subscriptions — your customers move through the exact same five stages. Most businesses stumble through each one on instinct. We built software that tracks every step.

Electronics Repair Legal & Consulting SaaS & Software Laundry & Dry Cleaning Automotive Healthcare Trade & Construction
01
Phase One

Identified

Someone enters your world. A quote is requested, a form is filled, a phone rings. Whether you capture this moment systematically or let it slip is the first place businesses lose money — long before any work begins.

Car repair → vehicle booked in Legal → intake form submitted Software → trial sign-up Laundry → drop-off logged
02
Phase Two

Committed

The deal is struck. Both sides have said yes — and the terms are agreed. A deposit lands, a contract is signed, an invoice goes out. This is where scope creep and missed expectations are born if nothing is written down.

Car repair → job card + deposit Legal → engagement letter signed Software → subscription started Laundry → receipt issued
03
Phase Three

Fulfilling

The work is live. Hands are busy. This is the phase most businesses run entirely on gut feel — and where delays, miscommunication, and missed handoffs quietly cost money. Visibility here separates calm teams from reactive ones.

Car repair → on the ramp Legal → drafts in review Software → configuration underway Laundry → in process
04
Phase Four

Delivered

Value changes hands. The job is complete, the keys are returned, the final invoice is cleared. This moment should feel like a milestone — not just the end of a transaction. Done right, it plants the seed for the next one.

Car repair → collected + signed off Legal → documents handed over Software → system goes live Laundry → garments collected
05
Phase Five

Closed

The loop is sealed — deliberately, not by accident. A follow-up lands. A review is requested. The next conversation is seeded. Most businesses skip this phase entirely and wonder why repeat business feels random. This is where loyalty is built, or quietly abandoned.

Car repair → satisfaction call Legal → end-of-matter letter Software → NPS survey sent Laundry → review request triggered
